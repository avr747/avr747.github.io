The MLIS program at the University of Missouri has 4 Student Learning Outcomes (SLO's) that graduate students need to meet in order graduate. Each SLO is described in the table below with a direct link to view a project I completed to demonstrate my mastery of it. More indepth information about a Project can is also be accessed through the dropdown menu under "Projects".



Student Learning Outcomes (SLOs)
SLO Goal Assignment
SLO 1: Foundations of Information Professionals To ground students in history, values, ethics, and guiding principles of the information professions. Graduates will apply the core principles, ethics, values, and body of knowledge to questions in library and information science, according to their area of specialty. View
SLO 2: Management of Informatioon Organizations To provide students with the evaluative and analytical skills to respond flexibly and adaptably to future changes and challenges within the organizations and institutions where they will be employed. View
SLO 3: Technology To provide students with opportunities to develop cutting-edge technological skills and competences used across the information professions.Graduates will be competent in information organization and lifecycle activities techniques of organization of information and with using and evaluating technologies of the information professions. View
SLO 4: Access, Use, & Users To develop an appreciation of the service-based and user-focused nature of the information professions. Graduates will be able to assess community needs, formulate plans to respond to users of information agencies, and instruct users in using informational resources. View
