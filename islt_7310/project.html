The MLIS program at the University of Missouri has 4 Student Learning Outcomes (SLO's) that graduate students need to meet in order graduate. Below you will find four projects that I have completed during my time in the program that meet a specific SLO. More in depth information on each project can be found in the dropdown menu under "Projects".


SLO 1:Foundations

Advocacy Letter to Congressman

For this SLO, the project I've chosen demonstrates mastery by applying the core principles, ethics, values, and body of knowledge to questions in library and information science according to their area of specialty.


SLO2: Management

Digitization Project

This project demonstrates my ability to apply management principles and interdisciplinary best practices in a library and information agency.


SLO3: Technology

ArchivesSpace Report

My project for SLO 3 demonstrates competentcy in information organization and lifecycle activities techniques of organization of information and with using and evaluating technologies of the information professions


SLO 4: Access, Use, & Users

American Folklore LibGuide

The Project for SLO 4 demonstrates my ability to assess a community need, formulate a plan to respond to users of information agencies, and instruct users in using informational resources.
